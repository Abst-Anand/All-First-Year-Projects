Our History & Achievements

History

We are a Brahmin Family. Our ancestors loved peace.
Although our ancestors were poor, but they had established high-level social and moral values.
They did not had enough food grain to eat properly twice a day. They were forced to have sugarcane istead of proper meal.
Inspite of this situation, they helped many needy people around them with open heart.They were full of Daya and Karuna. In our family, we have two freedom fighters, Pt.Prabhunath Tiwari (Lalaji) and Pt.Shiv Shambhu Tiwari (Bhola Ji).

Also, Pt.Kailas Nath Tiwari (Tyagi Ji) was constable in Hathua State and helped both the freedom fighters in many ways.

Lalaji:
He was hard and dry-honest social worker and worked for the development of society.
Those activities led him to become MLA from Bihar Assembly Elections in 1965.
He had two sons, Satya Prakash (Ajay) who was a lawyer in Gopalganj Civil Court later on he became Judge at Jamshedpur,Tatanagar. And
his second son, Gyan Prakash (Vijay) is professor at Hathua Sanskrit College.

Tyagi Ji:
He has two sons Upendra Nath Tiwari and Bhupendra Nath Tiwari. And a daughter, Durgawati.

Upendra Nath Tiwari: Due to poor financial condition, went to gulf and made the family financially stable.

Bhpendra Nath Tiwari: Studied at Prayagraj (Allahbad), completed diploma in Computer Science and
then went to Kuwait to further strengthen economical status of the family.
In his chilhood, he felt that Dowry is curse to society and pledged to educate his children so high that no one has to ask for Dowry.
He has three daughters Sneha, Tanushree, Garima and one son Anand.
